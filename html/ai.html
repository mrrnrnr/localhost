Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence

Technologies move forward every year. All scientists are engines of progress, including IT specialists. Why don't we become one of these geniuses and create our own artificial intelligence?

Explaining his definition, John McCarthy points out: “The problem is that we cannot yet generally determine which computational procedures we want to call intelligent. We understand some mechanisms of intelligence and do not understand others. Therefore, intelligence within this science refers only to the computational component of the ability to achieve goals in the world."

About the course

The Artificial Intelligence course is designed for children from 7-10 years old. It will help you get to the first stage of programming.

The program is designed for 5 months of intensive study. During this time, the course will help you learn how artificial intelligence works. At the beginning of the lesson there will always be a small knowledge test, so to speak, a warm-up, to refresh your memory. Professionals will work with the children, some even students who can find a common language with the guys.

What skills will the child receive during the course?

In the process of studying this course, children will gain skills such as:

  1. programming skills
  2. mathematical thinking
  3. logical thinking
  4. algorithmic thinking
  5. team work skills
  6. creative thinking
  7. problem solving skills
  8. communication skills

These skills will become the basis for further study of technical sciences.

Course program

To learn more, check out the detailed course program.

The modules of the Artificial Intelligence course include:

  1. The study of images, which is created by Artificial intelligence
  2. Creating images on the Playground platform
  3. Communicating with characters controlled by Artificial Intelligence
  4. Introduction to the concept of "Computer Science"
  5. Introduction to the concept of "Logic"
  6. Introduction to the concept of "Robotics"
  7. Introduction to the topic "Neural networks"
  8. Introduction to the topic "Machine Learning"
  9. Introduction to Scratch and Micro:bit programs

Each block of four lessons ends with a project lesson, where students show their capabilities after gaining new knowledge about the course. Also at the end, the children must make a common project, for all nine months of study.
